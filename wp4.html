5 Description: Objectives: Implementation of TSO framework developed in WP2 to characteristic case studies. Description of Work: The TSO framework developed in WP2 will be applied for the optimized design of characteristic case studies, including steel structural elements, connections and simple structures. Task 4.1 Design optimization: In this Task: (i) Members of varying cross-section will be optimized, such as hollowsection pylons of stay and back-stay cables in suspended roofs and Isection, long-span bridge girders. The end geometry of the members to accommodate their connections will be included in the optimization process. (ii) Joints between steel members of varying orientation, cross-sectional shape and action effects will be optimized. The sections of adjoining members will be used as imposed constraints, to which the geometry of the joint will have to adapt. (iii) Simple structural systems, such as trusses for pedestrian bridges or industrial applications will be optimized, taking into account aesthetic and/or geometry constraints. The constitutive relations obtained in WP3 will be employed in the optimization process. Task 4.2 Parametric Design for Design Optimization: In Task 4.2, the application of Parametric Design principles, that are used as the platform of operation towards the design optimization of structures, and related case studies will be reviewed and discussed further, suggesting in parallel possible directions of investigation. Parametric Design allows the effective design development of any complex and non-standard architectural and structural system55. Within this framework, Parametric Design tools will be applied for: a) 3D modelling and geometrical development of suggested structural morphologies, b) Morphological and behavioral control of structures during the process of optimization, c) as well as in view of the fabrication technique to be applied.

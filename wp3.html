1 Work package number: WP3
2 Work package title: Determination of material constitutive relations for 3D printed steel using recycled powder
3 Starting Month: 1
4 Ending Month: 18
5 Description: Objectives: The main objective of WP3 is to define the material constitutive relations that need to be introduced in the optimization procedure that will be carried out in WP4. Description of Work: The mechanical properties of the 3D printed objects, among others, are highly affected by the consumables used, especially in case of recycled powder feedstock. Moreover, the GMNIA-based design procedure that is part of the nonlinear TSO methodology developed in WP2 relies on the material properties considered, thus a reliable description of the material constitutive relations is highly important. In this WP the determination of the material constitutive relations is achieved by two Tasks. Task 3.1 Production, processing and classification of the recycled powder: The production, processing, sorting and dispatching of the most suitable powder for AM technologies is a promising field. In this Task using the metal powder pelletizer of the “Interuniversity Center for the Development of High Value-Added Processes and Products from the Recovery/Exploitation of Waste” of NTUA lower quality metal powders will be converted to high value-added raw materials suitable for AM processing. Task 3.2 3D printing of specific steel specimens including recycled consumables: In this task using the upgraded recycled powder of Task 3.1 and conventional consumables appropriately scaled 3D printed coupons will be manufactured in appropriate shape, size and number, as specified by EN10002-1. In particular 10 steel specimens will be manufactured, half of them using conventional consumables and the other half with recycled ones. Task 3.3 Experimental testing: Then tension tests (10 specimens: 5 with conventional and 5 using recycled consumables) will be performed at the Institute of Steel Structures of NTUA in order to determine material constitutive relations, using the available infrastructure, including strong floor, testing frames, actuators and measuring devices, properly connected to a centralized control system. Results will be compared with test results of 3D printed steel that are available in the recent literature. Guidelines for numerical modeling of material nonlinearity will be formulated. Task 3.4 Calibration of material constitutive relations: In order to identify the parameters of the material constitutive laws for steel and for each type of consumables adopted (conventional and recycled) a material law will be developed based on inverse analysis carried out by means of a metaheuristic-based search algorithm (Kallioras et al. 201853), for this reason parameterized constitutive relations will be chosen from the literature in order to be calibrated through inverse analysis procedure (Chamekh et al. 200654).

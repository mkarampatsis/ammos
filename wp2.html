2 Work package title: Development of TSO framework incorporating GMNI FEM analyses
3 Starting Month: 1
4 Ending Month: 18
5 Description: Objectives: The topology-sizing optimization methodology based on nonlinear FE analyses will be developed in WP2. Description of Work: A completely novel topology-sizing optimization framework will be developed, in which the capability of performing Linear Buckling (LBA, Dimopoulos and Gantes 201251) and GMNI Analyses as well as treating multiple loading cases in each iteration will be incorporated. Task 2.1 nonlinear TSO methodology: In this Task the proposed Nonlinear Analyses based Topology-Sizing Design Optimization methodology will be developed, that is composed of three stages: In the first one, the TO approaches will be used, improved in terms of computational efficiency (see Task 2.2); they will be based on the classical elasticity theory for continuous media and simple stress constraints, but taking into account multiple loading combinations (the design loading conditions). This will lead to roughly optimized shapes of the structural elements (members, nodes) or structures. In the second one, labelled as the interpretation stage, the optimized shapes resulted from the first stage will be interpreted into CAD designs. More specifically, for structural elements, joint or structures, 3D CAD designs and then FE discretized models will be created automatically, for truss-like structures an automatic procedure will also be implemented generating truss structures, verifying also that the produced trusses will not correspond to a mechanism. The resulting interpreted design composed of tubular or rod structural elements will be used as starting configurations for the third stage that will comprise a sizing design optimization procedure based on buckling and nonlinear analyses, more information on the design procedure is provided in Task 2.3. Through these iterations the roughly optimized shape of the first stage will be further optimized so that all verifications against all pertinent failure mechanisms will be included in the analysis. This approach has obvious advantages, as the final optimized shape at the end of the third stage will require no further interpretation or additional checks to be manufactured. Task 2.2 parallel implementation of the methodology: In this Task the high computational demands of the nonlinear TSO methodology will be treated into a parallel computing environment52 taking advantage of the progress on CPU and GPGPU hardware. Task 2.3 Design of steel members, connections and structures with non-linear FEM analyses: In order to design steel members, connections and structures with non-linear FEM analyses, a Linearized Buckling Analysis (LBA) will first be performed, in order to obtain buckling mode shapes that will then be adopted as initial imperfections for the subsequent Geometry and Material Nonlinear Analysis with Imperfections (GMNIA). Such analysis incorporates all verifications against all pertinent failure mechanisms and is a valid alternative to code-based design for conventional structures, while it is the only solution for structures with unconventional geometry.
